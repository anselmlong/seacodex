# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Marketplace operators, growth analysts, and listing owners evaluating how a Shopee product listing may perform across Singapore buyer segments. They are in an analytical workflow: tune listing inputs, compare projected sales and chatter, and identify listing changes worth making.

## Product Purpose

WLIAS is a dashboard demo for simulating product listing contagion across Singapore buyer networks. The name is short for "we live in a society", matching the product's focus on networked buyer behavior. Success means users can understand how listing content, pricing, shipping, vouchers, urgency, and positioning change projected sales, objections, and segment-level response.

## Brand Personality

Analytical, practical, locally aware. The interface should feel like a working market lab, not a marketing page or decorative concept mockup.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboard gloss, decorative glassmorphism, overly rounded cards, heavy gradients, and category-obvious e-commerce templates that hide the actual simulation task.

## Design Principles

- Keep controls close to their consequences so users can tune inputs and immediately see what changed.
- Prioritize legible decision support over decorative novelty.
- Make Singapore-specific segment behavior visible, concrete, and scannable.
- Use familiar product UI affordances for forms, controls, charts, and playback.
- Treat simulated outputs as directional evidence, not absolute forecasts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, keyboard access, focus visibility, semantic structure, and reduced-motion-safe interactions. Color-coded charts and state should not be the only way to understand meaning.
